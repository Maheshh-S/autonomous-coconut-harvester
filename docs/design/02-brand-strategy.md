# 02 — Brand Strategy

> Companion to `01-design-constitution.md`. Defines ARECA's name, voice, logo direction, and
> brand world. No code, no assets yet. Every choice reinforces the autonomous-coconut-
> harvesting identity from §1 of the constitution.

---

## 1. Name

**ARECA** — a real genus of tropical palms (Areca), echoing the coconut family without
being the literal word "coconut." It reads as a proper product name, is short, ownable, and
pronounceable. It signals *botanical precision* rather than *robotics hype*.

- **Spoken:** "uh-REE-kuh" or "AIR-uh-kuh" — both fine; the brand guide uses **AH-reh-kah**
  in voiceover for the documentary clips.
- **Never** written as "Areca" mid-sentence in a way that reads like a plant genus in copy;
  as the product word it is **ARECA** (caps) in UI, sentence-case "Areca" only in prose.
- Tagline (used sparingly, never as an eyebrow on every section):
  **"The plantation, autonomously harvested."**

---

## 2. Voice & Tone

| Context | Voice |
|---|---|
| Landing hero | Calm, declarative. One sentence that states what ARECA is. |
| Dashboard | Status, plain. "12 trees ready. Mission running." |
| Survey | Discovery. "The drone found 302 trees across 10 tiles." |
| Twin | Topographic, factual. "This is the surveyed plantation, as captured." |
| Robot | Operational. State-led: "Climbing. Harvesting. Returning." |
| History | Recounted, resolved. "Harvested 47 mature coconuts. 18 min." |
| Trees | Careful, specific. Per-tree health, not aggregate fluff. |

**Words we use:** plantation, tree, drone, survey, tile, twin, mission, robot, climb,
harvest, mature, inventory, coverage, yield.
**Words we avoid:** leverage, supercharge, seamless, smart (filler), powerful, magic,
next-gen, AI (as a noun bragged about — say what it *does*: "detects", "plans", "harvests").

**Numbers:** real or explicitly mocked (`<!-- mock -->`, "sample"). No `92%`/`4.1×`
invented precision (taste-skill §4.9).

## 3. Logo Direction

The logo must communicate **coconut plantation + autonomous survey** with no words.

### 3.1 Primary mark (recommended)
A **single coconut frond / leaflet** rendered as a clean geometric mark, with a small
**drone-view reticle** nested at its base — the leaf = the plantation, the reticle = the
machine vision that sees it. Constructed, not illustrated. Monochrome in the accent or
cream; never a photo, never emoji.

- Geometry: the leaflet is a near-symmetric spine with 5–6 paired offshoots; the reticle is
  a thin circle + crosshair occupying the negative space at the leaf's base.
- This is the **one** hand-built mark allowed (taste-skill §3.C permits "a single, simple
  geometric mark"). All *other* icons come from a real library (Phosphor/Tabler).

### 3.2 Wordmark
`ARECA` set in the display sans (see `03`), tight tracking, no gradient, no glow. The
wordmark alone is sufficient on the nav bar; the leaf-reticle mark leads sections that need
a glyph.

### 3.3 Forbidden marks
- ❌ A coconut (whole/full) as a literal emoji-style icon.
- ❌ A generic robot head / humanoid.
- ❌ A drone with a slash or "no" treatment.
- ❌ Gradient or neon renders of the mark.
- ❌ Wordmark with gradient text (constitution §10).

## 4. Brand World (the visual territory)

A single, consistent *documentary film* of an autonomous coconut plantation:

- **Time of day:** golden hour and soft overcast morning — warm, real light. Not midnight
  neon, not high-noon harsh.
- **Palette anchor:** deep canopy shade (near-black green-charcoal) + leaf green + husk/
  coconut-cream + drone-scan cyan (functional accent only).
- **Texture:** bark, frond, soil, tile-mosaic seams. Real, photographed — never fake-noise
  gradients.
- **Motion:** slow, deliberate, machine-steady. A drone doesn't jitter; the robot doesn't
  rush. Calm authority (constitution §3).
- **People:** absent from product imagery. The system is the subject. (If a human appears,
  they are off-frame / contextual only.)

This world is codified for video in `10-google-flow-guidelines.md` and for shots in
`06-shot-list.md`.

---

## 5. Brand in the UI (where identity shows without text)

Even with the leaf-reticle mark hidden, the UI must read as ARECA:

1. **The twin mosaic** *is* the brand — a real surveyed plantation, not a chart.
2. **Drone-scan cyan** appears only where the machine is "seeing" (active scan, live robot,
   selected tree) — a semantic accent, not decoration.
3. **Photographic heroes** on every primary page use real plantation footage (clips from
   `assets/clips/`), never gradient blobs.
4. **Tree cards** show real detection boxes / maturity — the CV is visible, not hidden.
5. **The robot** is shown as a climbing harvester doing the work — the product's proof.

---

## 6. Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Lead with the plantation imagery | Lead with a feature grid |
| Name things for what they are (Trees, Survey) | Use vague umbrellas (Tools, Home) |
| Use one accent for live/active state | Spread accent across badges/buttons/headers |
| Real numbers or labeled mock | Invent `4.1×` precision |
| Constructed leaf-reticle mark | Emoji coconut / humanoid robot |
| Calm, factual voice | Marketing hype ("supercharge") |
