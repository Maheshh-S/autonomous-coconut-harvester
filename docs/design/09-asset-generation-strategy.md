# 09 — Asset Generation Strategy

> Defines WHICH assets ARECA needs, WHERE they are used, and HOW each is produced. No
> generation in this phase. Every decision reinforces the autonomous-coconut-harvesting
> identity (constitution §1) and the brand world (§02 §4). Explicitly rejects AI-slop by
> choosing the right medium per asset.

---

## 1. Principles

1. **The product is photographed, not illustrated.** Real plantation footage > generated >
   procedural > abstract.
2. **Medium matches the job** (taste-skill §4.8 priority order, adapted): video clips from
   Google Flow (Veo) for heroes/ambient; real backend survey tiles for the twin; SVG/
   primitives for diagrams; Three.js only if a true 3D need emerges (none required for v1).
3. **Never AI-generate the identity marks.** The leaf-reticle logo is constructed, not
   prompted. Survey tiles are real drone photos from the backend.
4. **Consistency over convenience.** All clips read as one documentary film (§10).
5. **No cyberpunk / neon / humanoid / warehouse** in any generated asset (constitution §10).

## 3. Medium decisions (explicit)

### Google Flow / Veo videos — A1–A7
- The 7 documentary clips (§06). Generated by the user in Google Flow from the improved
  prompts in `assets/prompts/` (standardized after §10). Dropped into `assets/clips/`.
- **Never** use these for: logos, icons, diagrams, UI chrome.
- **Always** loop-seamless for ambient (S2/S6/S7); play-once for hero/orientation (S1/S4/S5).

### Real photography — B1, B2
- Survey tiles and inspection images come from the **backend** (real drone + ripeness
  models). Not generated, not faked. This is the source of truth for the twin.
- Frontend renders them via the frozen tile endpoints; no synthetic imagery.

### Constructed / SVG — C1, D1, D2
- The leaf-reticle mark is the **only** hand-built mark allowed (taste-skill §3.C). All other
  icons are from the library. Diagrams are SVG built from primitives or a chart lib, themed
  to the palette. No decorative hand-rolled SVG.

### Procedural — F1
- Grain/texture only as a fixed, non-scrolling overlay; optional; off by default.

### Three.js — none required (v1)
- The twin is a real photo mosaic + DOM overlay (frozen, performant at ~300 trees). No 3D
  engine needed. If a future need arises (thousands of trees → WebGL), revisit — but it must
  preserve `onSelectTree` / `selectedTreeId` and `computeMosaicLayout` (constitution §9.1).

---

## 4. Assets that must NEVER be AI-generated
- The leaf-reticle logo (constructed).
- Survey tile + inspection imagery (real backend).
- Icons (library).
- Diagrams (SVG primitives).
- Any UI chrome, buttons, cards (components, not images).
- Fake screenshots / div-based mockups (taste-skill §4.8 banned).

AI generation is confined to the 7 atmospheric documentary clips (A1–A7) where real footage
is impractical to shoot.

---

## 5. File & folder convention
- Clips: `assets/clips/<shot>.mp4` (filenames fixed in §06).
- Prompts: `assets/prompts/<NN>-<shot>.md` (improved set, this phase).
- Real imagery: served by backend tile/inspection endpoints (no local copy needed).
- Logo/diagrams: `frontend/public/` or inline SVG component (decided at impl).

---

## 6. Consistency gate
Before any clip is accepted: it must match the master Google Flow guidelines (§10) — same
camera language, color grade, lighting, no forbidden elements. A clip that looks like a
different film is rejected and re-prompted.
